Repair work

One of the B-2 Spirit models broke during student lab experimentation and had to be repaired cleanly enough to return it to use.

Repair method

The split carbon fiber was injected with cyanoacrylate and hand-clamped for about 10 minutes. Aircraft-grade structural adhesive was then added to the tip, allowed to cure, sanded smooth, blended with acrylic paint, and sealed with a thin 1:4 hardener-to-epoxy mixture.

Why it mattered

This repair showed that the model could be restored and returned to lab use rather than discarded, which is important for student-handled equipment used repeatedly in the Mechanical Vibrations laboratory.

Spare-model production

Continuation of the project

After the original models proved useful in the Mechanical Vibrations laboratory, Dr. Hemanth Porumamilla asked that spare B-2 Spirit models be created for continued lab use.

Current status

Three models were completed during Summer 2024, and additional spare models are being produced using the same general composite process.
