Mechanical Vibrations Lab Carbon Fiber Wet Layup Mass Properties Equipment

Fabrication, finishing, repair, and spare production of a carbon fiber B-2 Spirit model

This website summarizes the design and manufacturing work for an upgraded B-2 Spirit model used in the Cal Poly Mechanical Vibrations Mass Properties laboratory. The older aircraft model was replaced by a larger 3.5-foot wingspan version built from a 3D printed PLA core and a carbon fiber outer shell.

See fabrication steps

Project overview

The main purpose of this project was to improve the aircraft-shaped object used in the mass properties lab. The model had to be larger, stiffer, and better looking while still being practical for repeated student use. The manufacturing process used a 3D printed PLA core, extensive sanding and surface preparation, carbon fiber wet layup, vacuum bag curing, trimming, finish sanding, and a thin final epoxy coat.
